Zagallo They said it would be impossible to field all those No10s

Published 14 Jan 2024

FIFA takes a light-hearted look at which songs, films, sportspeople and inventions were en vogue in December 2007.

If Pele is the Humphrey Bogart, and Rinus Michels the Martin Scorsese, then Mario Zagallo is football’s Clint Eastwood – a true virtuoso at both performing and directing. The trailblazing winger hadn’t kicked a ball for his country one month before Sweden 1958 began, but somehow got Vicente Feola to denounce the age-old, flair-dependent Brazilian script and started every game, before helping Seleção retain gold Chile 1962. Zagallo was then the filmmaker as his all-star crew made cinematic gold at Mexico 1970. The ‘Old Wolf’ nevertheless hunted more. If Katharine Hepburn could become the only person to touch an Oscar four times, he could do the same with football’s most prestigious prize. He duly lifted the FIFA World Cup™ for a still-record fourth time as Carlos Alberto Parreira’s insightful assistant at USA 1994. On the 50th anniversary of the Azetca apogee of ‘The Beautiful Team’ in 2020, Zagallo, who passed away in January 2024, chatted to FIFA about that tournament, his playing days and more.
